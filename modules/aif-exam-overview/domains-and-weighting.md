# The Five Domains and Their Weighting

The exam guide splits AIF-C01 into five **domains**, each with a weighting that reflects how many scored questions come from it.

## The domains

| Domain | What it covers | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Fundamentals of AI and ML** | Core terms (AI, ML, deep learning), types of ML, the ML lifecycle | **20%** |
| **2. Fundamentals of Generative AI** | Foundation models, tokens, embeddings, prompts, use cases and limits | **24%** |
| **3. Applications of Foundation Models** | Prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, model selection and evaluation | **28%** |
| **4. Guidelines for Responsible AI** | Fairness, bias, transparency, explainability, safety | **14%** |
| **5. Security, Compliance, and Governance** | Securing AI data and models, governance, compliance | **14%** |

They sum to 100%. Weightings are guidance for an average exam; your specific set of questions is drawn randomly.

## Read the weights strategically

- **Domains 2 + 3 are 52% of the exam.** Generative AI and foundation models, in practice mostly **Amazon Bedrock**, are where most of your study time should go. Prompt engineering, RAG, and fine-tuning show up a lot.
- **Domain 1 (20%)** is the vocabulary and mental model everything else builds on. Don't skip it.
- **Domains 4 and 5 (14% each)** are smaller but high-yield: responsible AI and security concepts are concrete and easy to lock in, easy points if you study them, easy losses if you don't.

## The exam is conceptual, not hands-on

Because it's foundational and aimed at users rather than builders, questions test whether you can:

- explain a concept in plain terms,
- pick the **right AWS AI service** for a business scenario,
- reason about when generative AI is (or isn't) the right tool.

You won't be asked to write code or tune hyperparameters.

## Key takeaways

- Five domains: AI/ML basics (20%), GenAI basics (24%), foundation models (28%), responsible AI (14%), security & governance (14%).
- Generative AI + foundation models are 52%, prioritise Bedrock, prompting, RAG, and fine-tuning.
- Questions are conceptual and scenario-based: match the service to the use case.
