# Exam Format and Scoring

Knowing the mechanics up front removes surprises and shapes how you practise.

## The numbers

- **65 questions**: **50 scored** and **15 unscored**. The unscored ones are trials for future exams; you can't tell which is which, so treat every question as if it counts.
- **90 minutes** total.
- **Foundational** level, aimed at people who use AI/ML on AWS but don't necessarily build it. No coding.
- **100 USD**, valid **3 years**.

## Question types

AIF-C01 uses four formats:

- **Multiple choice** - one correct answer, three distractors.
- **Multiple response** - two or more correct out of five or more options; you must select **all** correct answers, no partial credit.
- **Ordering** - put 3-5 items in the correct order (e.g. steps of the ML lifecycle).
- **Matching** - match a set of responses to 3-7 prompts (e.g. match each AWS service to its use case).

Ordering and matching are newer formats, they reward knowing *sequences* (like the ML lifecycle) and *service-to-use-case* pairings, both of which this course drills.

## Scoring

- Results are a **scaled score from 100 to 1000**; you need **700** to pass.
- Scoring is **compensatory**: you pass on your **overall** score, not per domain. A weak domain can be offset by strong ones, though the heavily weighted domains (2 and 3) still matter most.
- **Unanswered questions are scored as incorrect**, and there's no penalty for guessing, so **answer everything**.

The exam is pass/fail. Aim for a comfortable margin above 700 in practice, not perfection.

## Key takeaways

- 65 questions (50 scored), 90 minutes, pass at 700/1000, 100 USD.
- Four question types: multiple choice, multiple response, ordering, matching.
- Compensatory scoring: only the overall result must pass.
- Never leave a question blank.
