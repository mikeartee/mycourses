# Exam Format and Scoring

Knowing the mechanics of the exam removes surprises on the day and shapes how you should practise.

## The numbers

- **65 questions**, of which **50 are scored** and **15 are unscored**. The unscored questions are being trialled by AWS; you can't tell which is which, so treat every question as if it counts.
- **130 minutes** total, roughly **2 minutes per question** on average.
- Delivered at a Pearson VUE test centre or via online proctoring from home.

## Question types

There are two formats:

- **Multiple choice** - four options, exactly **one** correct.
- **Multiple response** - five or more options, **two or more** correct, and the question tells you how many to pick.

**There is no partial credit.** On a multiple-response question you must select all the correct answers and none of the wrong ones to earn the point. These take longer, budget extra time for them.

## Scoring

Your result is a **scaled score from 100 to 1000**, and you need **720 to pass**. Because it's scaled (not a raw percentage), you can't map "I got 45 of 50" directly to the scaled number, but roughly 72% correct is the ballpark target. Aim higher than 720 in practice exams to leave a safety margin.

The exam is **pass/fail**. A distinction between "just passed" and "aced it" doesn't appear on your certificate, so don't chase perfection, chase a comfortable, reliable pass.

## What this means for how you study

- **Practise under time pressure.** Two minutes per question is generous until you hit a wordy scenario. Full-length timed mock exams build the pacing muscle.
- **Flag and move on.** You can mark a question for review and come back. Don't sink 6 minutes into one item early on.
- **Every question is scenario-based.** You won't be asked "what is S3." You'll be given a situation and asked for the best design given constraints like cost, latency, or availability.

## Key takeaways

- 65 questions (50 scored), 130 minutes, ~2 min each.
- Two formats: multiple choice (one answer) and multiple response (pick the stated number); no partial credit.
- Scaled score 100-1000, pass at 720.
- Practise timed, flag hard questions, and expect scenarios rather than definitions.
