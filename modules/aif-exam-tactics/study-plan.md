# A Study Plan and Resources

AIF-C01 is foundational, most people can prepare in a few weeks. Here's a practical plan.

## A rough 2-to-4 week plan

1. **Week 1: Fundamentals.** Domain 1 (AI/ML basics, the lifecycle) and the AWS AI service landscape. Get the vocabulary solid.
2. **Week 2: The heavy middle.** Domains 2 and 3, generative AI and foundation models. This is 52% of the exam. Really understand Bedrock, prompt engineering, RAG vs fine-tuning, and inference parameters.
3. **Week 3: Responsible AI + security/governance.** Domains 4 and 5, smaller but high-yield. Learn the tool-to-goal mappings (Clarify, Guardrails, Macie, CloudTrail, Artifact).
4. **Week 4: Practice.** Timed practice exams; review **every** question, right or wrong, until you know why each option is correct or not.

## Get hands-on

The single best thing you can do: open a free-tier AWS account and **try Amazon Bedrock and PartyRock**. Write prompts, add a Knowledge Base (RAG), and toggle temperature. Ten minutes of playing teaches prompt behaviour better than pages of notes.

## Where to study

- **AWS official**: the exam guide, AWS Skill Builder (free digital courses and an official practice question set), and the AWS Cloud Practitioner Essentials for baseline cloud knowledge.
- **AWS documentation and AI Service Cards**: concise, exam-relevant, and authoritative.
- **Reputable practice exams** with explained answers.

## What to prioritise

- **Bedrock and generative AI** (Domains 2-3): the biggest slice.
- The **keyword-to-service mapping** across all the AI services.
- The **tool-to-goal mappings** for responsible AI and governance.
- The **ML lifecycle** order (for ordering questions).

## Before you book

Book when your **timed practice scores** sit comfortably above 700. Because it's foundational, once the concepts click and you can match services to use cases quickly, you're ready.

## Key takeaways

- Sequence: fundamentals -> generative AI + foundation models (the 52%) -> responsible AI + governance -> timed practice.
- Get hands-on with Bedrock and PartyRock; it makes the concepts stick.
- Use official AWS resources (exam guide, Skill Builder) and review every practice question.
- Book once timed scores are comfortably above 700.
